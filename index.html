Brand architecture

You do not need lots of extra brands yet.

The cleanest model is a master brand, one umbrella studio surface, and only a small number of standalone product names where they truly help. Treat Elusion as the root idea, Elusion Works as the operating layer, and let product names earn their place one by one.

Tier 1

Elusion

The root brand idea: digital creation, invention, transformation, and interactive craft.

Tier 2

Elusion Works

The umbrella operating brand and the public roof over websites, hubs, tools, and games.

Tier 3

Named properties

Keep distinctive names only where the audience, function, or market is genuinely different.

Keep

Distinct product names like AI Resource Hub, Axy Lusion, and Synthetic Dispatch where they feel like real destinations.

Avoid

Inventing lots of middle-layer sub-brands before there is a business reason, a separate audience, or a separate revenue stream.

Use later

Create new sub-brands only when a product becomes independent enough to warrant its own marketing and identity system.

Default move

If something is just a capability, service line, or internal tool, keep it under Elusion Works rather than naming it like a separate company.

About

A digital studio that should feel sharp, clever, and alive.

Elusion Works should read like a serious creative-technology business, but it should never feel dead, generic, or corporate. The site needs to feel crafted.

That means strong hierarchy, deliberate motion, interesting mechanics, and a visual language that feels premium without collapsing into bland startup design.

# Elusion Works Brand Architecture

## Recommended structure

- `Elusion Works`
  The umbrella operating brand and public-facing label. This is the broad company layer that can cover websites, hubs,
  tools, games, and future services without sounding boxed into one category.

- `Elusion`
  Keep this only as internal brand shorthand or conceptual language, not as the standalone public label across the websites.

- Named properties
  Keep distinctive public names only where they help real audiences understand a clear
  destination or product.

## What should sit under Elusion Works

These should usually stay as products, properties, or internal systems rather than spinning out
into standalone brands:

- websites
- hubs
- tools
- pipelines
- experiments
- internal systems
- future service lines

## When to create a new brand

Create a more distinct brand only when one of these becomes true:

- it serves a meaningfully different audience
- it has its own marketing identity
- it has a separate revenue model
- it needs independence from the wider estate
- the name itself is helping growth rather than adding noise

## Practical naming rule

If something is a capability, operating function, or shared engine, keep it inside
`Elusion Works`.

If something is a destination people will visit directly and remember by name, it can earn a
stronger standalone property name.

## Current recommendation

- Public umbrella site: `Elusion Works`
- Internal shorthand only: `Elusion`
- Property names to keep where they already serve a clear public destination:
  `AI Resource Hub`, `Axy Lusion`, `Ghost in the Model`, `Kol's Korner`

This keeps the estate legible while still leaving room for stronger product identities later.
